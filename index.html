what do (research summary)

i think a lot about the predictive models we use for sequences with the hopes that this effort will enable better methods development in genomics and protein engineering. within computational biology, i do a bit of "methods" work, but i think of my research more as "models for methods" if that makes any sense. my modeling philosophy is fairly syncretic and originates in statistics, natural language processing, and parallel computing

given health inequities and current research efforts, i believe it important to prioritize fairness in modern methods development, and i also believe that equity and inclusion efforts are integral parts of modern research environments. in addition to fairness as a research interest, i work with the Bias Busters RSO at UC Berkeley to better understand and build inclusive environments, and im always happy to chat about DEI-related issues in academia and research :)
